Case Study : Identity Theft 

Identity theft is a type of cybercrime where an attacker steals a person’s personal information and uses it without their permission, often for financial gain or fraud. This information may include Aadhaar details, bank account numbers, passwords, or social media credentials. 

In a typical scenario, the attacker first collects personal data through methods like phishing emails, fake websites, or data leaks. Sometimes, users unknowingly share sensitive information on untrusted websites or public platforms. Once the attacker has enough information, they may create fake accounts, access the victim’s bank account, or apply for loans in the victim’s name. In some cases, the attacker may even impersonate the victim on social media to scam others. 

College students and working professionals are common targets, especially those who frequently use online platforms but may not follow strong security practices. People who reuse passwords or share personal details publicly are at higher risk. 

The consequences of identity theft can be serious. Victims may face financial loss, damage to their reputation, and difficulty in recovering their accounts. It can also take a long time to fix issues like unauthorized transactions or fake profiles. 

This case shows the importance of protecting personal information, using strong passwords, and being cautious while sharing data online.
